11-seven fuel lock

always wanted to get the cheapest fuel in your area, even beating out costco’s prices for unleaded 98? this is your guide to achieving 11-Seven’s cheapest price across the country. you can guarantee that you’ll save hundreds of dollars in the long run!

this method is based on running an emulator on your PC, and to trick the app into thinking that you’re physically near the cheapest fuel lock location.

initial setup - rooting

  1. Install Nox (https://en.bignox.com/pg/android9-beta)
  2. Nox Assist > Add emulator > Android 9 (Premium)
  3. Click on the Settings Cog (⚙) next to the your new emulator instance > General > tick Root under Startup items
  4. Run the emulator
  5. Download Magisk Delta, then drag the APK into the emulator to install it (https://huskydg.github.io/magisk-files/)
  6. Open Magisk, grant it Superuser access
  7. Restart the emulator instance by going back to Nox Assist, hitting the stop icon, then launching it again
  8. Open Magisk Delta, click Install under next to Magisk (usually near the top), click Next, then use Direct Install into system partition
  9. Reboot when prompted
  10. Shut down the emulator, then undo the Root option from Step 3
  11. Run the emulator
  12. Download the LSPosed Zygist module, then drag it into your emulator window (https://github.com/LSPosed/LSPosed/releases) This will open the Amaze File Manager; grant file access to Amaze, then drag the LSPosed ZIP file into a folder you can easily locate again
  13. Open Magisk, go to the Modules tab, click Install from storage, then select your LSPosed ZIP from wherever you saved it
  14. Reboot when prompted

installing and hiding your location

  1. Download and install the 11-Seven and Fake GPS apps
  2. Open Magisk, go to the settings menu, enable Zygisk and MagiskHide. You do not need to restart yet
  3. In that same settings menu, find Configure MagiskHide then select My 11-Seven
  4. Install manager.apk located in the LSPosed ZIP downloaded in Step 11 - you may have to extract the ZIP to your PC first
  5. Open LSPosed, head to the Repository tab, search for Hide Mock Location, click Releases, click Assets, and click on the HideMockLocation APK to download it into the emulator
  6. Install HideMockLocation by clicking on it from the notifications bar
  7. Enable HideMockLocation by heading to LSPosed > Modules, then clicking on HideMockLocation
  8. Scroll down and tick the My 11-Seven App
  9. Go back to Magisk > Settings > Hide Magisk app and proceed with prompts; name it anything you’d like
  10. Enable mock locations in the Developer Options
  11. Head to the emulator’s Android settings > System > About tablet/phone > Click on Build number seven (7) times to enable Developer Options
  12. Go back one page, Advanced > Developer options > Select mock location app > Fake GPS
  13. Restart the emulator

installing and hiding your location

  1. Head to refinery.fyi on your PC and click on your fuel of choice to copy its coordinates
  2. Open the Fake GPS app, hit the menu in the top left > Go to > paste your coordinates
  3. Hit the play button (▶) in the bottom right
  4. Head to the My 11-Seven App app and lock in!

miscellaneous

By default, opening Nox again would open an emulator instance that has none of your hard work saved, but don’t fret, it’s still in your multi-instance manager! To skip the annoying step of having to launch the right instance simply:
  1. Open the multi-instance manager and close all running emulations
  2. Delete the instance on level 0
  3. This one will usually be a few hundred megabytes, and the one you’ve spent your time on will be about a gigabyte
  4. Copy our My 11-Seven App instance, then delete the original
From this point, Nox should default to the correct instance upon first open
If the My 11-Seven App app requires an update, simply download an updated APK and drag it into the emulator
